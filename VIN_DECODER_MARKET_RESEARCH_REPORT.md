## 3. AVAILABLE APIs & DATA SOURCES

### 3.1 NMVTIS (National Motor Vehicle Title Information System)

**Overview:**
- Federal government database (DOJ)
- Aggregates data from state DMVs, insurance, salvage auctions, recyclers
- Most authoritative title/brand information
- Required for dealers in some states (California)

**Pricing:**
- Application Fee: $1,000 (non-refundable)
- Development Cost: $30,000 (one-time, at contract execution)
- Per-VIN Fees: Volume-based single rate structure
  - Low volume: ~$1.00/lookup
  - High volume: ~$0.25/lookup
- Consumer Reports via Providers: $3.49-$10.00

**Access Methods:**
1. Become approved provider ($31,000 startup cost)
2. Use existing provider's API (VinAudit, Auto Data Direct, etc.)

**Data Quality:**
- Government-verified title data
- Brand information (salvage, rebuilt, flood, etc.)
- Total loss records
- Odometer readings from title transfers
- Junk/salvage auction records

**Limitations:**
- Limited accident details (only total loss)
- No service/maintenance records
- No minor accident data
- State reporting varies

### 3.2 NHTSA vPIC (Free Government API)

**Overview:**
- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration
- Free, public VIN decoder
- No registration required
- 24/7 availability

**Pricing:**
- FREE (rate limited by traffic control)

**Data Provided:**
- Vehicle specifications (make, model, year)
- Body type, engine, transmission
- Safety features
- Manufacturer information
- Plant information
- Model year
- Vehicle type/class
- Partial VIN decoding supported
- Batch decoding available

**Formats:**
- JSON, XML, CSV

**Limitations:**
- No history data (accidents, owners, service)
- No title information
- No theft records
- Specifications only
- Rate limiting on high volume

**Best Use:**
- Free tier for basic VIN decoding
- Reduce data costs by using for specs
- Combine with NMVTIS for complete solution
